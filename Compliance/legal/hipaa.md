INTENT:
"Design, implement, and maintain HIPAA-compliant processes to ensure the protection, confidentiality, and integrity of Protected Health Information (PHI) within healthcare and related organizations."

CREATE:
"Create policies, procedures, and documentation that govern the collection, storage, use, and disclosure of PHI, ensuring compliance with HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules."

BUILD:
"Build systems, workflows, and controls that enforce HIPAA requirements, including access management, encryption, audit logging, and employee training to prevent unauthorized PHI access or disclosure."

PROCESS:
"Workflow:
    
    STEP-1:
    'Identify all PHI collected, stored, processed, or transmitted within the organization.'

    STEP-2:
    'Classify PHI according to sensitivity and regulatory requirements.'

    STEP-3:
    'Implement administrative safeguards: policies, training, workforce clearance, and contingency planning.'

    STEP-4:
    'Implement physical safeguards: controlled access, secure storage, device protections, and facility monitoring.'

    STEP-5:
    'Implement technical safeguards: access controls, encryption, audit logs, and secure transmission protocols.'

    STEP-6:
    'Establish breach detection, response, and notification procedures.'

    STEP-7:
    'Document all HIPAA compliance activities, risk assessments, and corrective actions.'

    STEP-8:
    'Regularly review, update, and test HIPAA controls and workflows.'"

ALTER:
"Allow HIPAA policies, processes, and systems to be updated based on regulatory guidance, risk assessments, and operational changes. Document all updates for compliance audits."

MAINTAIN:
"Maintain HIPAA compliance through continuous monitoring, training programs, regular audits, and incident management."

RULE:
"Ensure PHI confidentiality, integrity, and availability
Limit access to authorized personnel
Secure data in transit and at rest
Train staff regularly on privacy and security
Document and report all breaches promptly"

MANAGE:
"Manage HIPAA compliance by tracking PHI inventories, risk assessments, access logs, training records, and breach incidents."

MONITOR:
"Monitor access to PHI, system logs, data transmissions, and compliance gaps proactively."

PROTECT:
"Protect PHI through encryption, secure storage, access control, auditing, and incident response plans."

COLLABORATE:
"Collaborate with legal, compliance, IT, healthcare operations, and third-party vendors to ensure consistent HIPAA adherence."

EXECUTE:
"Execute HIPAA compliance practices to safeguard patient information, minimize risk, and maintain trust with patients, regulators, and stakeholders."
