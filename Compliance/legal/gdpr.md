INTENT:
"Design, implement, and maintain GDPR-compliant processes to ensure the protection of personal data of EU citizens, safeguarding privacy, and meeting regulatory obligations across all data processing activities."

CREATE:
"Create policies, procedures, and documentation that ensure lawful data collection, processing, storage, and sharing while respecting user consent, data subject rights, and transparency requirements."

BUILD:
"Build systems, workflows, and controls that enforce GDPR principles including data minimization, purpose limitation, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, confidentiality, and accountability."

PROCESS:
"Workflow:
    
    STEP-1:
    'Identify all personal data processed, stored, or transmitted across the organization.'

    STEP-2:
    'Classify data by sensitivity and purpose of processing.'

    STEP-3:
    'Establish lawful bases for processing data and obtain explicit consent where required.'

    STEP-4:
    'Implement privacy by design and default in system architectures.'

    STEP-5:
    'Create mechanisms for data subject rights: access, rectification, deletion, portability, and objection.'

    STEP-6:
    'Document data processing activities in a Record of Processing Activities (RoPA).'

    STEP-7:
    'Implement data breach notification procedures and reporting timelines.'

    STEP-8:
    'Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing activities.'

    STEP-9:
    'Regularly review and update GDPR compliance measures.'"

ALTER:
"Allow GDPR-related processes and policies to be updated based on regulatory changes, audit findings, and business process evolution. Ensure all updates are documented."

MAINTAIN:
"Maintain GDPR compliance through regular audits, training, monitoring of data flows, and continuous improvement of privacy practices."

RULE:
"Ensure lawful, fair, and transparent processing
Minimize data collection to what is necessary
Secure personal data at all stages
Respect and enable data subject rights
Document and demonstrate compliance continuously"

MANAGE:
"Manage GDPR compliance by tracking consent records, data processing activities, DPIAs, data breach incidents, and training completion."

MONITOR:
"Monitor data access, processing activities, system logs, and regulatory updates to proactively detect compliance gaps."

PROTECT:
"Protect personal data using encryption, access controls, anonymization/pseudonymization, and secure storage. Establish incident response plans for breaches."

COLLABORATE:
"Collaborate with legal, IT, security, operations, and business teams to ensure GDPR requirements are integrated across all functions."

EXECUTE:
"Execute GDPR compliance practices to ensure lawful data processing, reduce regulatory risk, and maintain trust with EU data subjects."
